## Color Considerations

- Use Dark Garments (instead of regular programs) when you want to protect blacks and deep colors and reduce visible detergent marks; it uses a higher water level, three rinses, and reduced spin to rinse thoroughly and wash more gently [ebooks.miele.com](http://ebooks.miele.com.au/miele-docs/miele_laundry_guidebook/files/assets/common/downloads/publication.pdf)
- Expect higher residue risk on dark loads when detergents contain zeolites; Miele specifically recommends detergent without zeolites for dark textiles and notes liquid detergents usually contain no zeolites [miele.co](https://www.miele.co.uk/support/customer-assistance/washer_dryer-1244/all/laundry_has_white_residues-92998142731)
- Separate whites when you need maximum brightness or you’re washing at high heat; NHS guidance for higher-risk hygiene situations also centers on hotter washes that many colors cannot tolerate [swbh.nhs](https://www.swbh.nhs.uk/wp-content/uploads/2024/01/Washing-patients-laundry-at-home-ML5331.pdf)

Mixed loads (what actually matters)
- Risk 1: Dye transfer, especially new dark items, and saturated reds and blues  
- Risk 2: Lint transfer, towels shedding onto darks  
- Risk 3: Uneven drying and wrinkling, heavy cottons crushing light synthetics

## Soil Level And Hygiene Choices

- Use Express only for lightly soiled items; Miele describes Express programs as intended for quick processing of lightly soiled laundry [ebooks.miele.com](http://ebooks.miele.com.au/miele-docs/miele_laundry_guidebook/files/assets/common/downloads/publication.pdf)
- Increase “program strength” before increasing temperature when the issue is abrasion, wrinkles, or color care, and raise temperature when the issue is oils, body soil, or hygiene  
- Reserve 60°C+ for genuine hygiene needs or heavily soiled household textiles; NHS leaflets advise washing contaminated or soiled items at a minimum of 60°C (or higher if fabric allows), and NHS hospital guidance similarly calls for 60°C+ in higher-risk contexts [nth.nhs](https://www.nth.nhs.uk/resources/washing-patients-soiled-clothing/)
- Avoid assuming 60°C guarantees sterilization; research summarized by De Montfort University reports that even 60°C settings can fail to remove some bacteria from textiles in testing, so stain removal, proper dosing, and drying matter too [dmu.ac](https://www.dmu.ac.uk/about-dmu/news/2025/april/home-washing-machines-fail-to-remove-important-pathogens-from-textiles.aspx)

Pre-treatment that saves loads
- Act fast: Flush stains from the back side with cold water for blood, dairy, and many protein stains  
- Pre-treat heavy grease: Work a small amount of liquid detergent into the spot, wait 10–15 minutes, then wash  
- Avoid heat-first: Skip hot drying until the stain clears, because tumble heat can set many stains

## Detergent, Softener, Tips, Mistakes, Special Cases

Detergent and softener
- Prefer liquid detergent (or any detergent without zeolites) for dark loads when you see white marks; Miele ties residue on dark fabrics to zeolites and notes liquid detergents usually contain no zeolites [miele.co](https://www.miele.co.uk/support/customer-assistance/washer_dryer-1244/all/laundry_has_white_residues-92998142731)
- Limit fabric softener on towels; Miele warns conditioner can reduce towel absorbency, especially if used every time [derkern.miele.co](https://derkern.miele.co.uk/2024/02/13/how-to-wash-towels-and-sheets/)
- Prevent stiff towels by rinsing fully; Miele notes detergent residue buildup can make towels hard and rough after drying [derkern.miele.co](https://derkern.miele.co.uk/2024/02/13/how-to-wash-towels-and-sheets/)

Top 2–3 practical tips
- Preserve dark color: Use Dark Garments for deep shades, and avoid zeolite-heavy powders that can leave visible residue [miele.co](https://www.miele.co.uk/support/customer-assistance/washer_dryer-1244/all/laundry_has_white_residues-92998142731)
- Keep whites bright: Use Whites (75) or Whites (90) for sturdy whites only, and avoid mixing with dye-risk colors when washing hot [swbh.nhs](https://www.swbh.nhs.uk/wp-content/uploads/2024/01/Washing-patients-laundry-at-home-ML5331.pdf)
- Maintain towel absorbency: Skip regular softener, rinse thoroughly, and avoid detergent buildup [derkern.miele.co](https://derkern.miele.co.uk/2024/02/13/how-to-wash-towels-and-sheets/)
- Prevent shrinkage: Favor lower dryer stress, pull items from Cottons programs promptly, and avoid overdrying (especially cotton knits) [media.miele](https://media.miele.com/downloads/82/a6/00_F6154D09FD151EECB8B51576CE1682A6.pdf)
- Reduce wrinkles: Use Shirts in the washer, use Shirts or Minimum Iron in the dryer, and hang immediately after the cycle ends [codex99](http://www.codex99.com/miele/W1_program_guide.pdf)

Common mistakes to warn against
- Overloading: Cuts rinse quality, traps detergent, and increases wrinkles  
- Overdosing detergent: Drives residue, stiffness, and dull-looking darks; residue problems on dark fabrics often show up as white marks [miele.co](https://www.miele.co.uk/support/customer-assistance/washer_dryer-1244/all/laundry_has_white_residues-92998142731)
- Tumble drying stains: Sets many stains permanently

Special cases
- New clothes (first wash): Wash dark new items separately or with other darks to reduce dye transfer risk  
- Stained items: Pre-treat, then wash, then air-check before drying  
- Smelly gym clothes: Wash sooner, avoid softener buildup, and dry fully  
- Zips/buttons/hooks: Zip, button, and hook everything, and turn items inside out to reduce snagging and abrasion
